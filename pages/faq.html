Having multiple lender relationships means we're not locked into one program — we find what works best for your specific situation.

It varies by loan program. As a general guide:

  • Chattel loans (home only): Some lenders start at 575–600
  • FHA loans: Typically 580+
  • USDA Rural Development: Typically 640+
  • VA loans: Typically 580–620+
  • Conventional: Generally 680+

Don't let a lower credit score discourage you from asking — we've helped customers get into homes in a wide range of credit situations. Get pre-qualified and let us see what's available.

A chattel loan finances the home only — treated like personal property (similar to a vehicle loan). These are common when you're placing the home on rented or leased land, or land you already own but don't want to tie to the mortgage. Rates are typically a bit higher, and terms are shorter.

A land-home loan (sometimes called a real property loan) finances both the home and the land together as real estate. These loans often have lower interest rates and longer terms — similar to a traditional mortgage. They typically require the home to be permanently affixed to the land.

Not sure which is right for you? Our team can walk you through both options based on your land situation.

Home Types & Models
5 questions

Single-wide homes are typically 14–18 feet wide and delivered as one unit. They're more affordable, easier to place on tighter lots, and perfect for individuals, couples, or small families.

Double-wide homes are two units joined together on-site — usually 24–32 feet wide. They offer more living space, more floor plan variety, and feel similar in size to a traditional site-built home.

Triple-wide homes are three sections joined together, offering the largest floor plans — ideal for large families who want maximum space. They require a larger lot for delivery and setup.

We carry homes from four top manufacturers:

  • Clayton Homes — One of the largest and most trusted names in manufactured housing
  • Deer Valley Homebuilders — Premium craftsmanship and upscale interior options
  • OwnTru (TRU Homes) — Modern designs at accessible price points

Manufactured homes are built entirely in a factory to the federal HUD Code and transported to the site on a permanent chassis. They are classified as personal property unless permanently affixed to land.

Modular homes are also factory-built, but they're constructed to local state and building codes (the same codes as site-built homes) and delivered in sections to be assembled on a permanent foundation. They are classified as real property.

We primarily carry HUD-code manufactured homes. If you're looking for modular, ask our team — we can discuss what's available.

Delivery & Setup
4 questions

We handle delivery and setup in-house. That means fewer middlemen, tighter quality control, and real accountability — if something's not right, you call us directly and we fix it. We don't outsource this to random subcontractors you've never met.

Our standard setup includes:

  • Transport to your site
  • Blocking and leveling on your prepared pad
  • Permanent tie-down and anchoring per HUD standards
  • Joining sections (for double-wide and triple-wide)
  • Skirting installation around the perimeter
  • Steps at all entry doors
  • A/C unit installation and connection

Utility connections (water, sewer, electric) are typically handled separately by the buyer's contractor, as those require licensed tradespeople.

We do a thorough walkthrough with you after setup so any issues are identified immediately. Transit damage that's documented at delivery is covered — we'll coordinate with the manufacturer's warranty or our own team to make it right. We won't hand you keys to a home with open issues.

Delivery requires a clear path for a wide-load transport truck. As a general guideline:

  • At least 14–16 feet of clearance width on the access road
  • Adequate overhead clearance (no low-hanging power lines, tree branches)
  • A turnaround area or through-access on the property

If you're unsure about your site's access, send us your address or some photos and we can evaluate before you commit. It's better to know early.
